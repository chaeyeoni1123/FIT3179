Mortality Rate of Below Age of 5 from 2000 to 2019

The visualization below shows the mortality rate of below age of 5 from 2000 to 2019. As we can see from the data, it shows the mortality rate decreases over time.


Mortality Rate of Below Age of 5 by Income Class

The visualization shows the average mortality rate of below age of 5 based on income class. It shows that the higher income class, the lower the mortality rate, which means the relationship between income class and the mortality rate of below age of 5 is negative.

Death Rate of Below Age of 5 by Income Class

The visualization shows the type of top 10 death caues of below age of 5 based on income class. Communicable is the type that is able to spread and non-communicable is not able to spread. It shows that low income and lower middle income countries have high death rate of communicable diseases such as 'Parasitic and vector diseases' and 'Lower respiratory infections', which can be cured easily with high medical technologies. However, high income countries have high non-communicable diseases' death rate such as cancer and diabetes, which is considered hard to be cured.

Rate of Number of Doctors in 10,000 Population by Income Class

This visualization shows the average rate of the number of doctors in 10,000 population in each income class. As we can see from the chart, the higher income, the higher rate of the number of doctors. It is important to check the number of doctors, because as I said earlier, high medical technologies is one of the factors that brings a decrease in the mortality rate of below the age of 5.

Rate of Number of Nurse & Midwifery in 10,000 Population by Income Class from 2009 to 2017

This visualization shows the average rate of the number of nurse and midwifery in each income class over time. As we can see from the line connected between those income classes, higher income countries have higher rate of the number of nurse and midwifery.

Conclusion

These visualizations show that the mortality rate of below the age of 5 and income classes of countries have negative relationship, which means higher income class have lower mortality rate. Also from the visualizations, we found that there are some medical factors for the relationship. It is trying to say that income class must have positive relationship with those medical factors, because higher income class countries lead to high medical technologies. Then, those countries that have high medical technologies seem to have lower mortality rate of below the age of 5, which means income class has negative relationship with the mortality rate.
